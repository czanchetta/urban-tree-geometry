# Limitations

**This tool does not replace field measurement.** It produces preliminary
estimates for lighting pre-modelling only. It must **not** be used for
arboricultural reports, tree-removal (supressão), environmental compensation,
stability assessment, rigging/lifting design, or any legal or normative
decision.

## Principal limitations

- **No local calibration.** `H∞` and `k` are adopted from general literature,
  not fitted to trees at the site. No statistical confidence interval exists.
- **No measured heights.** Height is inferred from perimeter alone.
- **No measured crown diameters.** The crown layer is a heuristic; real crowns
  should be represented by two field-measured orthogonal diameters.
- **Pruning and urban conditions.** Pollarding, topping, crown loss, lean,
  competition and root restriction all alter the DAP–height–crown relationships.
- **Site effects.** Soil, water, light and management change adult size.
- **Crown asymmetry.** The circular X = Y assumption cannot represent real
  asymmetric or wind-shaped crowns.
- **Palms.** Height and crown for palms are weakly related to stipe diameter;
  confidence is low and field measurement is recommended.
- **Incomplete identification.** Genus/family-only records have low confidence.
- **Extrapolation.** Very large DBH (> ~120 cm) extrapolates beyond the adopted
  curves; treat with caution (e.g. UA50 in the source data).
- **Not for legal use.** For any laudo, supressão, compensação, estabilidade or
  hoisting decision, measure height in the field with a clinometer, hypsometer
  or topographic survey.

## Trees near luminaires
Trees close to lighting fixtures should be **measured in the field**; a
preliminary estimate is not sufficient for photometric decisions where the tree
directly obstructs or is illuminated by a fixture.
